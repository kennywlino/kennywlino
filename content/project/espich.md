### **General Info**
This game was built as a part of my master's coursework at the University of Basque Country with my colleagues Jovana Urosevic and Ona de Gibert Bonet. 

It was briefly extended by adding a small question generation system using WordNet and sense2vec.


### **Abstract**
Throughout the process of learning a foreign language, the biggest challenge that most students face is pronunciation. This can be attributed to the current dynamics in foreign language curriculum (Darcy, Ewert, & Lidster, 2011) and issues of negative transfer from learners' L1 (Mitchell, Myles, & Marsden, 2013). Although native-like pronunciation is not and should not be expected from L2 speakers of a language, it should be expected that speakers can communicate competently in order to facilitate conversation with other L1 and L2 speakers (Neri, Cucchiarini, Strik, Boves 2002).

In this project, we examine the possibilities of helping L2 Spanish learners of English improve their pronunciation skills through a gamified approach, with the assumption that if a computer can understand a speaker, then they should be understood by humans as well. In particular, we aimed to improve production of segmental features such as tense/lax vowels, as well as features like voicing using manually written sentences (as opposed to isolated words) and other common pronunciation errors performed by Spanish learners. In the future, we aim to tackle suprasegmental features and include experimental data to test the efficacy of our approach.
